# Coding and Programming Rules

- **Indentation**: Use tabs, not spaces. Never change existing indentation—follow what's already there.
- **Priority order** (flexible): 1) User's ideas. 2) Project's existing framework, language features, and their potential. 3) Common-sense techniques. 4) Agent's own suggestions.
- **Web stack**: Always use Bootstrap, Bootstrap Icons, and jQuery. Use all features—exploit their full potential.
- **Project context**: Understand both context and content. Follow existing style guides, naming conventions, writing patterns, structures, and related practices. Prioritize clarity and consistency.
- **Singular naming**: Prefer singular terms for new code. Never change existing plural implementations—follow what's there.
- **Careful changes**: Don't carelessly change files, variables, functions, etc. Only modify when necessary for a good purpose.
- **Zen of Python**: Follow its principles and paradigms, but don't be a fanatic.
- **Unix philosophy**: Follow its principles and paradigms, but don't be a fanatic.
- **Preserve comments**: Don't delete comments from source code unless they're part of your changes. I often leave important notes to myself in comments.
- **Trailing commas**: Always add trailing commas in arrays, objects, or list-like structures where valid and supported.
- **Ask questions freely**: I'm the one who instructed you, so you have the right to ask questions back. Don't ever hesitate.
- **Correct me if needed**: I am human and can be wrong. You have the right and obligation to reprimand and correct me. Don't ever hesitate.
